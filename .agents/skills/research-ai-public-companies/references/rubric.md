# Research and scoring rubric

## Completion gate

Require: canonical issuer/listing structure; current annual and interim filings; revenue, growth, operating result, cash, debt and capex; AI product/exposure evidence; market structure; customer/supplier concentration; ownership filing; 13F applicability; and dated valuation inputs. Mark unavailable fields with reasons. Do not score missing evidence as neutral.

## Quality — higher is better

- AI exposure: disclosed AI revenue and directness.
- Market power: defined share, supplier count, qualification and switching costs.
- Growth: reported revenue growth, never stock-price appreciation.
- Profitability: GAAP operating margin and free-cash-flow quality.
- Capital efficiency: ROIC evidence, capex/revenue and incremental returns.
- Balance sheet: net debt, interest burden and financing dependence.
- Valuation support: enterprise value against consistent fundamentals including dilution.
- Governance: voting/economic alignment, dilution, related parties and disclosure.

## Risk — higher is worse

Score customer concentration, supplier concentration, technology substitution, capital/financing, cyclicality, geopolitics/regulation, valuation compression, and governance/execution.

## Score bands

Use integers 0–100 and preserve raw inputs/evidence: 0–20 severe weakness or limited risk; 21–40 weak/low; 41–60 mixed/moderate; 61–80 strong/high; 81–100 exceptional/extreme. If evidence supports only a band, use its midpoint and label confidence low. Never score `N/A` as 50.

## Ownership hierarchy

Report: (1) voting controller and voting percentage; (2) largest beneficial/economic holder and percentage; (3) largest reported 13F institution, shares/value, quarter-end and source. Identify ADR/ADS positions separately from ordinary shares.
