# AI company radar charts

**Evidence date:** 2026-06-22  
**Coverage:** 17 companies with a dedicated analysis or dated market snapshot in the global AI industry report  
**Output:** one investment-quality chart and one risk chart per company  
**Confidence:** company-specific; shown on every chart and in the source dataset

## Reading the charts

The quality chart is directional: higher is better. The risk chart is also directional, but higher is worse. The charts are deliberately separate so that high AI exposure is not visually treated as the same thing as low risk.

Scores are **rubric-based research assessments**, not raw measurements, forecasts or target prices. A score of 75 does not mean a company has a 75% probability of success. The bands are:

| Score | Quality interpretation | Risk interpretation |
| ---: | --- | --- |
| 0–20 | severe weakness | limited identified exposure |
| 21–40 | weak | low |
| 41–60 | mixed | moderate |
| 61–80 | strong | high |
| 81–100 | exceptional | extreme |

The source-of-truth dataset is [`data/ai-company-radar.json`](../data/ai-company-radar.json). It contains every plotted value, ownership classification, reporting date, evidence key and confidence rating. Charts are generated by [`scripts/generate-radar-charts.mjs`](../scripts/generate-radar-charts.mjs); they are not edited by hand.

## Dimensions

### Investment quality — higher is better

| Dimension | Evidence used |
| --- | --- |
| AI exposure | disclosed AI/cloud/data-center revenue, product mix and whether the exposure is direct or an unquantified adjacency |
| Market power | defined product-market share, sole-source status, oligopoly structure and demonstrated switching costs |
| Growth | latest reported revenue growth and, where available, multi-year growth rather than share-price momentum |
| Profitability | operating margin, free-cash-flow quality and the gap between GAAP and adjusted results |
| Capital efficiency | capital expenditure required for incremental revenue, asset intensity, ROIC evidence and dilution |
| Balance sheet | cash, debt, interest burden and dependence on repeated external financing |
| Valuation support | dated market capitalization relative to revenue, earnings or free cash flow; lower multiples are not automatically better |
| Governance | voting structure, controller conflicts, dilution, disclosure quality and alignment with outside shareholders |

### Risk — higher is worse

| Dimension | Evidence used |
| --- | --- |
| Customer concentration | disclosed major-customer revenue and dependence on a small number of hyperscalers |
| Supplier concentration | sole-source components, foundries, GPU supply and critical manufacturing partners |
| Technology substitution | credible competing architectures, open standards, custom silicon and product obsolescence |
| Capital/financing | capex intensity, debt, converts, warrants and the need to fund capacity before revenue arrives |
| Cyclicality | memory, semiconductor, advertising, hardware inventory and capacity-cycle exposure |
| Geopolitics/regulation | export controls, antitrust, data sovereignty and geographic manufacturing concentration |
| Valuation compression | degree to which the current valuation depends on exceptional future growth |
| Governance/execution | controller risk, integration, delivery commitments, accounting controls and operational complexity |

Market capitalization and share price are displayed in the underlying industry report, not scored as “quality.” Size is not a shareholder return.

## Ownership and 13F treatment

“Largest holder” has two different meanings and the dataset preserves both:

1. **Controller/largest beneficial owner:** derived from the company's proxy, 20-F or equivalent ownership table. This determines voting power and control.
2. **Largest reported 13F institution:** the largest row in the dated Nasdaq institutional aggregation used for this report. It reflects reported U.S. long positions and may not be the company's largest global shareholder.

Form 13F is filed up to 45 days after quarter-end. It does not provide a synchronized live cap table, complete global ordinary-share ownership, short positions or a complete economic picture of derivatives. For ASML, Arm and TSMC, the 13F row covers the U.S.-traded security/ADS or ADR and must not replace the issuer's ordinary-share ownership disclosure.

## Reproduction and validation

Run:

```bash
node scripts/generate-radar-charts.mjs
```

The generator rejects values outside 0–100 and missing dimensions. A valid chart therefore proves that the JSON is structurally complete; it does not prove that the research judgment is correct. Low-confidence companies require additional filing-level work before using their scores for capital allocation.
